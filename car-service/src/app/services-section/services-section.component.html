Car Care Tutorials

Lesson 1

HOW TO CHANGE A TIRE

Steps Find a flat, stable and safe place to change your tire. Apply the parking brake and put car into "Park" position. Place a heavy object (e.g., rock, concrete, spare wheel, etc.) in front of the front and back tires. Take out the spare tire and the jack. Raise the jack until it is supporting (but not lifting) the car.

Lesson 2

HOW TO CLEAN BATTERY TERMINALS

Remove your battery cables, starting with the negative terminal first. Clean the terminal posts using a wire brush and corrosion removal tool. Save money and make your own mixture using a water and baking soda solution. Rinse the terminals with water and dry with a rag. Re-install your battery cables starting with the positive one first.

Lesson 3

HOW TO REPLACE AIR FILTER

The air filter box can be located in various places depending on your vehicle. A sure tale sign is there is a large tube coming from it and going to the motor. As a general rule of thumb, you should replace your air filters every 30 to 60 days. That is the recommendation of filter manufacturers of basic 1-3 inch filters. If you are prone to light to moderate allergies, you could benefit from a stronger air filter or change them more frequently.

Lesson 4

How To Check Your Coolant Level

Check your coolant level every time you refuel; Never remove the radiator cap when the engine is hot; It's not actually water in your engine, but coolant. Coolant, which is often green or red, contains chemicals that stop the liquid from freezing in sub-zero climates and prevents corrosion damage inside the engine.
